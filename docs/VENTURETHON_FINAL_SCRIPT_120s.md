## Judge-proof 120-second script

### [Hook | 10s]

---

### [Problem | 20s]
**"Today, a DAO treasury team jumps between Jupiter for swaps, Marinade for staking, a bridge for cross-chain moves, Squads for multisig approvals, Discord for coordination, and spreadsheets for tracking. That fragmentation creates delays and, worse, blind-signing risk—approving transactions without full context."**

---

### [Solution | 40s]
**"Keystone is the command layer for Squads-based treasuries. You type intent like: 'Swap 500 SOL to USDC, bridge half to Base, and stake the rest.' Keystone generates a step-by-step execution plan across those protocols. Then our Simulation Firewall simulates the full transaction bundle and verifies expected outcomes before you sign. You approve once, and Keystone executes the workflow."**

---

### [Proof | 20s]
**"We're at live MVP stage with native Squads v4 integration and end-to-end execution flows already built. This isn't a deck-first idea—we've shipped the system that turns intent into safe on-chain execution."**

---

**"DAOs are maturing into real financial operators, but their tooling is still click-ops. Keystone replaces it with command-ops. Next, Foresight simulation lets treasuries run 'what-if' scenarios on runway and risk, and Studio + Marketplace lets the ecosystem extend Keystone with plugins—so Keystone becomes the default operating layer."**

---

## Guardrails (do not break)

1. **Do not mention** active users, retention, conversion rate, 99.9% uptime, or "already serving users" until you can back it with numbers.
2. **If anyone challenges "verify expected outcomes"**, use this one-liner:  
   *"We simulate the full bundle and show the predicted deltas and instructions before signing."*
3. **Never say "mathematically verifies" or "mathematically proves"** unless you do formal verification. Stick to: *"simulates the full transaction bundle and verifies expected outcomes before you sign."*
