To execute one simple operation—like "rebalance our portfolio"—teams must:
- Open Jupiter for swaps
- Open Marinade for staking  
- Open Squads for multisig approvals
- Coordinate across Discord
- Track execution in spreadsheets

**The cost?** Lost opportunities, blind signing errors, and hours wasted on coordination.

**The market?** $350 billion in DAO treasuries, protocol funds, and crypto VCs—all struggling with the same fragmentation.

## 💡 The Solution (30 seconds)

**Keystone is the Command Layer for Web3 treasuries.**

Instead of clicking through five different tools, users describe their intent in plain English:

*"Rebalance to 60% SOL, 30% USDC, stake excess SOL for yield"*

**One command. One signature. Done.**

Our AI agents handle the planning; **our Simulation Firewall simulates the full transaction bundle and verifies expected outcomes before you sign.**

**But here's what makes us different:** We're not building another dashboard. We're eliminating dashboards entirely. We're the **CLI for the blockchain**—the operating system that makes Web3 treasury management as simple as typing a command.

## 📝 Delivery Notes

### Timing Breakdown (120 seconds total)
- **Hook**: 5 seconds
- **Problem**: 15 seconds  
- **Solution**: 30 seconds
- **Traction**: 30 seconds
- **Market**: 20 seconds
- **Ask**: 10 seconds
- **Closing**: 10 seconds

### Key Delivery Tips

1. **Start with confidence** - The hook must land immediately
2. **Paint the pain** - Make investors feel the fragmentation problem
3. **Show, don't tell** - Emphasize live products, not promises
4. **Numbers matter** - $350B market, 500+ DAOs, production-ready codebase
5. **Clear ask** - Flexible: Soonami EIR program or $2M for scale, not development
6. **End strong** - "Operating system" positioning is powerful

**Q: "What's your competitive advantage?"**  
A: "We're the only natural language command layer. Once teams adopt, switching costs are massive. Plus, we're non-custodial—we never touch funds."

**Q: "How do you monetize?"**

**Q: "What's your team?"**
